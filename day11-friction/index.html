Day 11 — Friction and Negative Acceleration

Friction always acts opposite an object's motion. As friction increases, the object's velocity decreases because friction produces a negative acceleration. Explore how changing the coefficient of friction changes the rate at which an object slows down, and connect this motion to the derivative of the velocity function.
