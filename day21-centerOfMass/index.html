Center of Mass

Every object, whether it's made of two blocks or billions of atoms, has a single point where its mass can be considered concentrated. Explore how changing the masses and positions of two objects shifts the system's center of mass, and discover how this familiar weighted average naturally extends into a calculus integral for continuous objects.
